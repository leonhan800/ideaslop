skateboard riser and razortail set

to protect against razor tails and prolong deck life/pop (debatable). baseplate reminescent of plastic slide addition on 2000s tensors. one recressed hole at tail end for wood screw attachment. requires 1" hardware for a tight fit with 0.2cm lift, 1.25" hardware results in 0.3cm lift. Dimensionsed for a steep 8.125" resin 7 dsm deck. Made for thunder team baseplate but compaitable with independents, nose slides have mixed bias between metal and plastic

2010 kia forte spare car keys (first car)

dimensions reconstructed digitially by scanning it with a office fax machine isometrically, works as intended but melted slightly due to summer heat

no-frills tekdek

simple tekdek, basic symmetrical nose and tail profile and no camber. wheel axle is inwardly bowed because i thought the weight of the users hand during operation would press down on the axle and would slide the wheels into place, in practice the wheels would fly off randomly during treflips and the the interface between the hangar and axle proved to be a major fracture point.

"butterfly" "knife""

very rudimentary, more of a proof of concept / brain tickler on how/if parts with high degrees of freedom can be printed in one-go. rotates smoother then expected, heavy break-in period that can be dramatically improved with graphite powder and light oil. performance/handling is poor because the part is too light even though it is balanced properly

better tekdek

improved version, wheels are static and has a cutout since the normal sliding friction is low and the cutout allows for easy tilting during the pre-pop stage allowing for easier rotatations and scooping. Chose a centered (vs off-set) groove since the wheels always allow for cross-lock 5050s. exaggerated geometries between the nose and tail, most notably the tail is recurved since the fiber orientation makes the deck a natural spring. Previous versions had camber but due to how this printed it caused alot of splitting and a soggy feeling (lack of spring force)
